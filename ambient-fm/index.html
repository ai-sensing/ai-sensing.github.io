AmbientFM: A Foundation Model for Ambient Intelligence Through WiFi

Preprint, February 2026

AmbientFM (Ambient Model — Foundation Model) is pre-trained on large-scale unlabeled WiFi CSI with contrastive learning, masked reconstruction, and physics-informed objectives, then adapted to diverse downstream sensing tasks with parameter-efficient fine-tuning.

Overview

Ambient intelligence—continuously understanding human presence, activity, and physiology in physical spaces—is central to smart environments, health monitoring, and human–computer interaction. WiFi offers a ubiquitous, always-on, privacy-preserving sensing substrate through billions of IoT devices. Yet wireless sensing has often relied on task-specific models that need substantial labeled data and are hard to deploy broadly.

We present AmbientFM, to our knowledge the first foundation model for ambient intelligence through WiFi. AmbientFM learns general-purpose CSI representations via contrastive learning, masked reconstruction, and physics-informed objectives suited to wireless propagation and multipath structure. The design includes adaptive frequency aggregation for heterogeneous subcarrier quality and relative temporal encoding for translation-invariant activity patterns.

Evaluated on public benchmarks spanning nine downstream tasks, AmbientFM shows strong cross-task performance and improved data efficiency, supporting the view that foundation-style pre-training can scale ambient intelligence on existing WiFi infrastructure.

Pre-training data

AmbientFM is pre-trained on 9.2 million unlabeled CSI samples collected over 439 days, using 20 commercial device types across 8 chipset families, 11 real-world environments, and 26 users, capturing diverse hardware, environments, and activities.

Downstream evaluation uses established public WiFi sensing benchmarks (nine tasks); details appear in the manuscript for per-task protocols and metrics.

Contributions

  1. Foundation model for ambient intelligence: We present the first large-scale foundation model for ambient intelligence with WiFi sensing, including architecture and pre-training design for CSI.
  2. Domain-specific pre-training at scale: We show that self-supervised learning tailored to wireless signals, on large real-world unlabeled CSI, yields representations useful for practical ambient perception.
  3. Single backbone, many tasks: We demonstrate strong generalization and data efficiency across nine diverse WiFi sensing tasks from public benchmarks via parameter-efficient fine-tuning.
